Manifesto

Power doesn’t just accumulate — it consolidates. Surveillance is no longer an exception or a scandal; it’s an operating system for modern institutions. Corporations monetize attention and behavior. States normalize monitoring. “Public safety” becomes an all-purpose excuse. And the price is metadata: who you talk to, when, where, how often — enough to map your life without reading a single message.

AnonChatX is built for people who can’t afford to be naive. Assume the network is watched. Assume disruption, blocking, and manipulation. Assume devices get searched. Assume contacts get pressured. When the internet is the threat, go offline. When the network is hostile, route through Tor.

Works online or offline

Different environments require different paths. AnonChatX supports multiple transports — and a fully offline way to share the app.

Transports

Choose the best path for the situation: Tor when the internet is usable, local links when the internet is the threat.

Tor (online)

Secure and anonymized routing over the internet. Bridges when Tor is blocked.

Wi-Fi (offline)

Communicate inside a local network with no traffic leaving the area.

Bluetooth (offline)

Proximity comms for situations without internet or Wi-Fi infrastructure.

External storage

Store-and-forward via SD card or file transfer for checkpoints and border crossings.

Threat model (pragmatic)

AnonChatX assumes that in serious cases: network traffic is monitored and contacts may eventually be compromised.

Assumptions
  • • Traffic from/to the device may be monitored.
  • • Blocking and manipulation may occur.
  • • Contacts may be compromised over time.
  • • Physical inspection is a realistic risk.
Design goals
  • • Avoid centralized servers that can be seized or subpoenaed.
  • • Provide offline options when networks are unsafe.
  • • Reduce metadata leakage in everyday workflows.
  • • Offer safety tools for coercive scenarios.

About iOS support

AnonChatX is Android-only. iOS imposes architectural and policy constraints that prevent providing the level of privacy, network control, and offline operation required by AnonChatX’s threat model. Supporting iOS would mean compromising on guarantees this project is not willing to weaken.

About Us

Our community and goals

We’re a global community of contributors with a shared goal: to keep private, resilient communication possible — even when power centralizes, networks are hostile, and trust assumptions fail.

AnonChatX is developed by people who believe privacy is not a feature you enable, but a condition you defend. Our focus is on practical tools for real-world risk: decentralized by design, usable offline, and honest about their limitations.

Support the Project

AnonChatX is an early-stage, independent project. It exists because some people believe private communication should remain possible even under pressure — and are willing to contribute time, infrastructure, or resources to make that happen.

Developers & contributors

Android, networking, cryptography, UX, documentation — contributions of all sizes matter. Review code, propose improvements, or help stress-test assumptions.

Financial support & Tor infrastructure

Infrastructure, hardware, testing devices, and long-term maintenance require funding. Financial support helps keep the project independent, sustainable, and operational.

Part of this support goes directly toward contributing to the Tor ecosystem by running relays and bridges — strengthening the network that millions of people around the globe depend on.

Sponsorship does not equal influence. AnonChatX is governed by its community — not sponsors, not donors, not institutions.
